Precipitation

There has been a small increase in precipitation across the United States over the past century. However, natural variability is high for precipitation, and it is difficult to attribute human activity over large swaths of area. This is because while there may only be a small increase in precipitation over the entire country, the extreme effects of different regions balance the averages out. As climate change progresses, the Northern states have seen large increases in precipitation, while the SouthWest has become much drier. Ohio isn't directly in an extreme zone of change, but the change shown in the chart will only accelerate if the use of heat-trapping emissions is not curbed.
