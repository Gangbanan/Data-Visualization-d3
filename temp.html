Temperature

The global temperature rise has been the clearest symptom of human-caused climate change. The average surface temperature of Earth has risen by about 2.0 degrees Fahrenheit since the beginning of the industrial revolution. Most of that rise has also happened within the last 35 years. While the number may seem small, this effect will only grow in the future. It has already had severe effects, especially the increasing activity of severe weather events and warming/rising oceans. Developing countries usually bear the most of these effects, but even the United States will have severe consequences from climate change in the future.
